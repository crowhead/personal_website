About Me

My name is Max Lewis, I'm a Full-Stack Web Developer with a background in visual design. Currently I am a part-time instructor at General Assembly in Washington, DC, where I teach a class in Adobe Illustrator for Beginners. At the end of this eight-hour course students gain essential Adobe Illustrator skills that are used in web development.

I currently live in Baltimore where I earned my BFA in Painting and Art History at the Maryland Institute College of Art. Since then I have learned Ruby, Ruby on Rails, HTML/CSS, Javascript, JQuery, Backbonejs, Reactjs, PHP, SQL, and WordPress at General Assembly in DC through their 12-week Web Development Immersive Program. I love painting in Adobe Photoshop with my Wacom, making art, brewing beer with friends, baking breakfast pastries, listening to Ruby Rogues Podcast, and running Dungeons and Dragons campaigns.

Global Champion

A collaborative project with four other team members. Global Champion is a multi-player Rock, Paper, Scissors game where I was in charge of most of the back-end development and created the vector images used in Adobe Illustrator.

DnDice Roller

DnDice Roller is a single-page app that makes playing Dungeons and Dragons lighter by removing the need to carry a bag full of dice. Make your saving throws and checks using DnDice Roller's intuitive design, success/failure messages, and slick aesthetics. This project was made using Ruby on Rails, Javascript, JQuery, HTML, and CSS.
